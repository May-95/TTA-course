New features of iOS 16.

By John Smith

The new features of iOS 16 were revealed during June's WWDC event. One of the main features is the ability to personalise the lockscreen with customisable font styles and widgets. You can now create different lock screen styles and switch between them. Widgets like the weather, time, battery levels and upcoming calendar events will allow you to get information at a glance without opening your phone.

Notifications now stay out of the way by appearing at the bottom of the screen. Changes have been made to Messages. You can now edit sent messages or unsend a message. Recently deleted messages can be recovered up to 30 days after deleting them. Messages can now be marked as unread for when you don't have time to respond but want to come back to it later on.

The health app has a new medication feature to help you manage and track your medication. iPhone users can now use the fitness app without an apple watch to track and meet their fitness goals. It uses the iPhone's motion sensors to count your steps and calculate an estimate of your calories to contribute to your daily Move goal.

The Notes app is getting some new features like the ability to create Quick Note in any app from the share menu. You can also now lock your notes with your iPhone passcode. Hidden and recently deleted albums in photos will be locked by default so only you can unlock it with your passcode. Live text is coming to videos. Text can now be interacted with in paused videos. You can copy, paste, look up or translate text in a video.

iOS 16 will be released sometime in September.
