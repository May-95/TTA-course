Maggie Smith

Summary

Margaret Natalie Smith is an English actress best known for her role as Professor Minerva McGonagall in the Harry Potter film series. She has had a long career on stage and onscreen, with over 60 films and 70 plays.

Early life

Margaret Natalie Smith was born in Ilford, Essex, on 28 December 1934 to Margaret Hutton and Nathaniel Smith. Her mother was a Scottish secretary from Glasgow and her father was a public-health pathologist from Newcastle upon Tyne. At four years old, the family moved to Oxford. Maggie Smith had older twin brothers. She attended Oxford High school which she left at age 16 to go study acting at Oxford Playhouse.

Career

Theatre

  • Maggie started her career at the Oxford Playhouse playing Viola in Twelfth Night. Some of the other Oxford Playhouse productions that Maggie acted in are Cinderella, Rookery Nook, Cakes and Ale, and The Government Inspector.
  • Between 1976-1980, Maggie appeared in Stratford Shakespeare Festival productions of Antony and Cleopatra, Richard III, and Macbeth.
  • She starred in the 1990 Broadway production of Lettice and Lovage which earned her a Tony Award for Best Actress.

Film

  • Maggie’s first film screen credit was Nowhere to Go as Bridget Howard. She received her first BAFTA Film Award nomination for this role.
  • For her role as Desdemona in Othello, she received her first Oscar nomination for Best Supporting Actress.
  • In 1969, Maggie won her first Oscar for Best Actress in The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie. She also won a BAFTA Film Award for the film.
  • Maggie won her second Oscar in 1978 for Best Supporting Actress for her role in California Suite.
  • She reprised her role as Violet Crawley for the Downton Abbey film which was a continuation of the TV series.
  • Maggie appeared in the popular Harry Potter film series as Professor Minerva McGonagall from 2001-2011.

Television

  • Maggie’s early television roles include All is Love, A Bed Among the Lentils, and Memento Mori. She received a BAFTA TV nomination for all three roles.
  • She portrayed Betsey Trotwood in the 1999 BBC television adaptation of David Copperfield. She received a BAFTA award and an Emmy nomination for the role.
  • In 2003, she played Mrs. Emily Delahunty in My House in Umbria. She won an Emmy Award for the role and a Golden Globes nomination.
  • She appeared in Downton Abbey as Violet Crawley from 2010 to 2015. Her role won her four SAG Awards, a Golden Globe Award, and three Emmys Awards.

Personal life

Maggie Smith married Robert Stephens on 29 June 1967 and had two sons. They divorced on 6 April 1975. Then, she married Beverley Cross on 23 June 1975. They remained married until his death in 1998. Maggie Smith has five grandchildren.

Maggie was made a Dame in the 1990 New Year Honours, for services to the performing arts. In 2014, Maggie was made Companion of Honour for services to drama.
